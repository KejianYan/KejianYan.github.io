Projects

Project 1: Fishing for Plastics: An Ethnography of Ocean Cleanup on a Chinese Sea Island

Conducted four months of fieldwork on marine debris governance on Changtu Island, tracing how fishermen, fisherwives, NGOs, tech companies, and governments collaborate and clash over the collection, classification, and monetization of ocean plastics.

Project 2: How Social Media Shapes Environmentalism in China

Based on digital ethnography of online environmental communities, argued that environmentalism is constantly enacted through netizens’ interactions and serves as a heuristic for them to rethink social hierarchy, guanxi, morality, and feminism. Different social media platform algorithms, however, may generate divergent forms of environmentalism and reinforce distinctions.
